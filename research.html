Science

Research

We integrate state-of-the-art AI with mechanistic models of the immune system to advance cancer immunotherapy and understand autoimmune disease.

Our lab bridges deep learning — transformers, structural predictors, generative models — with biophysics and computational immunology. A unifying theme is interpretability: we build models that are not only accurate but that reveal the biological rules behind their predictions, enabling mechanistic insight alongside computational power.

Research Area 01

Computational Immunology

We develop computational models to understand T and B cell function in the context of complex diseases — particularly cancer and autoimmune disorders such as rheumatoid arthritis. A major focus is decoding the specificity of T cell receptors (TCRs) and how B cells evolve in germinal centers.

We use mechanistic stochastic models to recapitulate B cell development and affinity maturation — including graph-based models of antibody affinity maturation — and deep learning models to predict TCR-epitope binding. We actively participate in community benchmarking challenges (e.g., IMMREP) to rigorously evaluate and improve these methods.

Research Area 02

Interpretable Deep Learning

Many deep learning models operate as black boxes — they achieve high predictive accuracy but provide little insight into why a prediction is made. This is a critical limitation in biology and medicine, where understanding mechanism is often as important as prediction.

We design architectures — including multi-modal attention-based networks and physics-informed models — that highlight the genetic and chemical patterns most informative for a given prediction. A key recent focus is fine-tuning large language models to decode immune recognition, making predictions interpretable at the level of amino-acid sequence features.

This line of work ensures our models can be trusted, interrogated, and used to generate hypotheses for experimental follow-up.

Research Area 03

Cancer Immunotherapy & Precision Medicine

A central goal of our lab is to advance the design of cell-based immunotherapies for cancer. We use pooled screening approaches combined with single-cell sequencing to dissect how different CAR-T cell signaling architectures affect T cell activation, persistence, and anti-tumor efficacy.

A major project focuses on T-cell structures and sequences in cancer and autoimmunity — leveraging structural AI alongside sequence-based models to identify the TCR features that govern antigen recognition and drive disease-relevant T cell responses.

We also develop multi-omics computational approaches for cancer patient stratification, integrating genomic, transcriptomic, epigenetic, and proteomic data to predict clinical outcomes and guide treatment decisions.

Research Area 04

Generative Protein & Drug Models

The recent revolution in protein language models and structural AI (AlphaFold, ESMFold, RoseTTAFold) opens new possibilities for immune receptor engineering. We develop and apply generative models for in silico design of antibodies, TCRs, and anticancer compounds with desired properties.

We combine deep generative models with molecular docking and multi-omics constraints to produce candidate compounds that are both chemically feasible and biologically motivated. A key emphasis is incorporating the genetic profile of target diseases — moving beyond generic generative approaches toward truly personalized drug design.
